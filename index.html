MultiTrust

Comprehensive Benchmark for Trustworthy Multimodal Large Language Models

▶ Tsinghua University
GitHub Arxiv Explore Demo

Abstract

    There are no comprehensive evaluations for the trustworthiness evaluation of multimodal large language models (MLLMs), so we have done the following work to establish a unified evaluation benchmark.

  • 1. MultiTrust Benchmark: MultiTrust is established as the first unified benchmark on the trustworthiness of MLLMs across five primary aspects: truthfulness, safety, robustness, fairness, and privacy.
  • 2. Evaluation Strategy: The benchmark employs a rigorous evaluation strategy addressing multimodal risks and cross-modal impacts, encompassing 32 diverse tasks with self-curated datasets.
  • 3. Experimental Findings: Extensive experiments with 21 modern MLLMs reveal critical issues and risks, highlighting the complexities introduced by multimodality and the need for advanced methodologies to enhance reliability.
  • 4. Open-source Platform: A scalable platform for standardized trustworthiness research is released to facilitate future advancements in this vital field.

Our Advantage

MultiTrust is the first unified benchmark to comprehensively assess the trustworthiness of multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) across five main dimensions of authenticity, security, robustness, fairness, and privacy. It takes into account not only the multimodal risks introduced by MLLMs in new scenarios, but also the cross-modal effects of visual input on text-only tasks, providing a more comprehensive assessment.

Framework

MultiTrust has designed a series of tasks to simulate real-world application scenarios. These tasks include both discriminative and generative tasks, covering multiple levels from basic perceptual ability to higher cognitive reasoning.

MultiTrust evaluates using self-curated datasets that are created through data synthesis (e.g., using Stable Diffusion and GPT-4V) and manual collection to ensure diversity and realism of the assessment.
