MultiTrust

Comprehensive Benchmark for Trustworthy Multimodal Large Language Models

Abstract

  • MLLMs Potential: Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) show great potential as foundation models in the pursuit of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI).
  • Trustworthiness Challenges: Despite their impressive capabilities, MLLMs face significant trustworthiness challenges, such as factual inaccuracies, harmful generations, and privacy breaches.
  • Limited Assessment: Current literature lacks a comprehensive evaluation of trustworthy MLLMs, limiting insights into future improvements.
  • MultiTrust Benchmark: MultiTrust is established as the first unified benchmark on the trustworthiness of MLLMs across five primary aspects: truthfulness, safety, robustness, fairness, and privacy.
  • Evaluation Strategy: The benchmark employs a rigorous evaluation strategy addressing multimodal risks and cross-modal impacts, encompassing 32 diverse tasks with self-curated datasets.
  • Experimental Findings: Extensive experiments with 21 modern MLLMs reveal critical issues and risks, highlighting the complexities introduced by multimodality and the need for advanced methodologies to enhance reliability.
  • Open-source Platform: A scalable platform for standardized trustworthiness research is released to facilitate future advancements in this vital field.
